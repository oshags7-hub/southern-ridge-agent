OUR ANIMALS & FARMING PRACTICES
================================================================================

Q: Are your cattle really grass-fed?
A: Yes — and we mean it. Our beef is 100% Angus, pasture-raised on our 180 acres
   here in Columbia. They graze on actual grass. We don't grain-finish them.
   "Grass-fed" has gotten watered down in marketing, so we want to be clear: our
   animals eat grass and forage from birth to harvest. No feedlot finishing.

Q: Do you use hormones or antibiotics?
A: No hormones, no antibiotics, no vaccines. That's our standard across all our
   animals — beef, pork, chicken, and lamb. We've built our herd and flock to be
   healthy without routine pharmaceutical inputs. If you ever have questions about
   a specific animal or product, just ask.

Q: What do your pigs eat?
A: Our pigs are woodland and pasture-raised, and their feed is corn-free, soy-free,
   and seed oil-free. We're intentional about what goes into the feed because it
   affects the fat quality of the pork. Low PUFA (polyunsaturated fatty acids) is
   something we pay attention to — it matters for the nutritional profile of the meat.

Q: What about the chickens?
A: Pasture-raised, corn-free, soy-free, seed oil-free. Same philosophy as the pork.
   Our chickens are outside, foraging, living the way chickens are supposed to live.
   The result is a more flavorful bird with a better nutritional profile — lower PUFA,
   better omega-3 to omega-6 ratio.

Q: Are you certified organic?
A: We're not certified organic. Organic certification is expensive and doesn't always
   reflect what actually matters to us — our standards go beyond what organic requires
   in some areas (like corn/soy-free feed and no vaccines) and we'd rather invest in
   the land and animals than in paperwork. What we can offer instead is transparency:
   come visit, ask questions, see for yourself how your food is raised.

Q: Can I visit the farm?
A: Yes, and we love it when you do. This is your farm too. We host farm meals, work
   days, concerts, and movie nights throughout the year. Follow us on Instagram
   (@southernridgefarm) or sign up for our newsletter at southernridgefarm.com to
   stay in the loop. You can also call us at (615) 636-7137 to ask what's coming up.

Q: What is regenerative agriculture?
A: It's farming that actively improves the land rather than just maintaining it.
   We're not trying to hold things steady — we're working to restore soil health,
   recharge waterways, and increase biodiversity on our 180 acres. That means
   rotational grazing, cover crops, and letting the land recover properly between
   uses. Healthy land produces better food. That's the whole point.

DAIRY QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: Do you sell raw milk?
A: We can't sell raw milk at retail — Tennessee law prohibits it. What we do offer
   is a herdshare program, which is the legal way to access raw milk in Tennessee.
   You own a share of our dairy herd, and we care for the animals on your behalf.
   The milk you receive is your share of production from animals you own — not a
   retail purchase. Call or email us to learn more about joining the herdshare.

Q: What is a herdshare?
A: A herdshare means you own a percentage of our dairy animals. We board and care
   for them on the farm. In return, you receive your proportional share of their
   milk each week. You're not buying milk — you own a piece of the herd. It's a
   written agreement, and it's the only legal structure for raw milk distribution
   in Tennessee. We have 250+ shareholder families — it's a real community.

Q: What is A2A2 milk?
A: A2A2 refers to the beta-casein protein in the milk. Cows with the A2A2 genetic
   variant produce milk with only the A2 protein — no A1. A lot of people who
   struggle to digest conventional dairy find they do much better with A2A2 milk.
   It's not about lactose — the lactose level is the same — it's the protein structure
   that's different. Our dairy herd is A2A2. We think it matters.

Q: Do you sell pasteurized dairy too?
A: Yes. We offer low-temperature vat pasteurized dairy products that don't require
   a herdshare — you can buy those directly. Low-temp pasteurization (145°F for
   30 minutes) is gentler than commercial high-heat pasteurization and preserves
   more of the milk's natural qualities. Available at The Ridge and online.

MEAT QUALITY & STORAGE
================================================================================

Q: How is your meat processed?
A: We process right here on the farm in our USDA-inspected facility. The whole
   supply chain is ours — we raise the animals, process them, cut them, and sell
   them. No middlemen, no third-party plants. You can trace your food from pasture
   to package without leaving our farm.

Q: How long will the meat keep in the freezer?
A: Vacuum-sealed cuts: 12 months or more. Ground beef: 3-4 months. Pork chops and
   steaks: 6-12 months. These are conservative guidelines — properly frozen meat is
   safe beyond these windows, though quality can decline. When in doubt, check for
   freezer burn and smell it after thawing.

Q: How should I thaw frozen meat?
A: Best method: move it to the fridge 24-48 hours before you plan to cook it.
   Cold water thaw works too — keep the package sealed and submerge in cold water,
   changing the water every 30 minutes. Don't thaw on the counter. Never re-freeze
   thawed meat unless you cook it first.

Q: Why does pastured meat sometimes look different from grocery store meat?
A: It's supposed to. Our beef is grass-fed, not grain-finished, so it's leaner with
   a different fat color — sometimes more yellow than white. That's beta-carotene
   from the grass. Our chickens have firmer, more flavorful meat because they actually
   exercise. Pastured pork can have a more complex flavor and slightly different fat
   composition. These are signs of quality, not problems.

Q: Do you offer dry-aged beef?
A: Yes. Dry-aged beef is available — check with us at The Ridge or online for
   current availability. Dry aging concentrates flavor and improves tenderness.
   It's worth asking about if you want the best steak on the table.
