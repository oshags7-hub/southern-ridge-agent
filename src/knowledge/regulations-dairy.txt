IMPORTANT: Raw milk retail sales are prohibited in Tennessee. Herdshare is the
ONLY legal pathway for consumers to obtain raw milk from this farm. Any agent
advising on dairy must understand and respect this constraint.

================================================================================
TENNESSEE RAW MILK LAW
================================================================================
Statute: TN Code § 53-3-119
Summary: Sale of raw (unpasteurized) milk for human consumption is prohibited
in Tennessee. The herdshare model is the recognized legal workaround.

Under herdshare, the consumer is not "buying" milk — they own a share of the
animal and are receiving their portion of the animal's production.

Penalty for violation: Civil penalty of $500 per violation per day.
Do NOT advise anyone to sell raw milk outside of a properly structured herdshare.

================================================================================
HERDSHARE REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================
A herdshare agreement must:
  1. Be a written contract between the farm and the shareholder
  2. Clearly state that the shareholder owns a specified percentage of the herd
  3. Specify the boarding/care fee paid to the farm (separate from milk "purchase")
  4. Acknowledge that the consumer assumes risk of consuming raw milk

The farm receives:
  - A boarding/care fee (this is what the farm is paid — NOT for the milk)
  - The boarding fee is what can be structured for regular payment

The consumer receives:
  - Their proportional share of milk production based on ownership percentage

Key distinction: the farm never "sells" milk. It boards animals on behalf of owners.

================================================================================
CONTRACT REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================
Every herdshare agreement should include:
  - Consumer's name and contact information
  - Percentage of herd owned by consumer
  - Species and approximate number of animals in share
  - Boarding fee amount and payment schedule
  - Acknowledgment of raw milk risks (signed)
  - Farm's name, address, and owner signature
  - Date of agreement

Recommended: Have a TN agricultural attorney review the template annually.

================================================================================
LABELING REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================
Containers of raw milk distributed to herdshare members should be labeled:
  - Farm name and address
  - "RAW MILK — NOT PASTEURIZED"
  - "For herdshare members only — not for resale"
  - Date of collection
  - Species (cow, goat)
  - [Confirm additional TDA requirements for herdshare labeling]

Do NOT label raw milk with a retail price or "for sale" language.

================================================================================
ADDITIONAL NOTES
================================================================================
- Herdshare members should not be listed as "customers" in retail records
- Boarding fees should be clearly separated from product sales in accounting
- Keep signed herdshare contracts on file indefinitely
- If a shareholder asks to "buy a share" on the spot at the farm stand, direct
  them to complete a formal written contract first — do not exchange milk informally
