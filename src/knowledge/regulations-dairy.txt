SOUTHERN RIDGE FARM — DAIRY REGULATORY GUIDE
Operational reference for compliance agents. Last updated: April 2025.

IMPORTANT: Raw milk retail sales are PROHIBITED in Tennessee. The herdshare
program is the ONLY legal pathway for customers to obtain raw milk from SRF.
Never advise anyone to "buy" raw milk outside of a properly structured herdshare.

================================================================================
1. TENNESSEE RAW MILK LAW
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
TN Code Annotated § 53-3-119 prohibits the sale of raw (unpasteurized) milk for
human consumption in Tennessee. This is a hard stop — there is no retail raw milk
license available in Tennessee as of April 2025.

WHAT IT MEANS IN PRACTICE:
  - SRF cannot sell raw milk as a product, period.
  - The herdshare model is the recognized legal structure: the customer owns
    a share of the animal and receives their portion of the animal's production.
    They are not "buying milk" — they are receiving production from their own
    animal that the farm boards and cares for on their behalf.
  - Boarding fees (what the farm charges) are legally distinct from milk sales.
  - If a customer at The Ridge or the farm stand asks to "just buy some raw milk,"
    the answer is: not possible. Direct them to the herdshare program.

PENALTY FOR VIOLATION:
  - Civil penalty of $500 per violation per day
  - Potential criminal charges and TDA enforcement action
  - Reputational and legal exposure for the entire operation

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - Written herdshare contracts on file for every active member (never verbal)
  - Contracts must be fully executed (signed by both parties) before any milk
    is transferred to the member
  - Keep signed contracts indefinitely

================================================================================
2. HERDSHARE PROGRAM STRUCTURE
================================================================================
HOW IT WORKS:
  - Customer enters a written herdshare agreement with SRF
  - Customer owns a specified percentage of the herd
  - SRF boards and cares for the animals on the customer's behalf
  - SRF charges a boarding/care fee (this is the farm's revenue — not milk sales)
  - Customer receives their proportional share of production

WHAT THE CONTRACT MUST INCLUDE:
  1. Customer's full name and contact information
  2. Percentage of herd owned by the customer (or number of shares)
  3. Species and approximate number of animals included
  4. Boarding/care fee amount and payment schedule
  5. Explicit acknowledgment that the customer owns a share of the animal,
     not that they are purchasing milk
  6. Signed raw milk risk acknowledgment (see section 3)
  7. Farm name, address, and owner's signature
  8. Date of agreement

WHAT TO AVOID IN CONTRACT LANGUAGE:
  - Never reference "selling milk," "purchase price of milk," or "milk price"
  - Never reference "per gallon" pricing in the contract itself
  - Boarding fee is per-animal share, not per unit of milk

  Active herdshare customers as of latest count: see admin dashboard

================================================================================
3. RAW MILK RISK ACKNOWLEDGMENT
================================================================================
WHAT IT MUST COVER:
Every herdshare contract must include a signed acknowledgment that:
  - Raw milk is not pasteurized
  - Raw milk may contain harmful bacteria including E. coli O157:H7,
    Salmonella, Listeria, and Campylobacter
  - Consuming raw milk carries health risks, especially for pregnant women,
    infants, the elderly, and immunocompromised individuals
  - The customer assumes all risk associated with consuming raw milk
  - The farm has not represented the milk as safe for all consumers

BEST PRACTICE:
  Have a TN agricultural attorney review the risk acknowledgment language
  annually. The legal standard for adequate disclosure may evolve.

4. RAW MILK LABELING AND HANDLING
================================================================================
LABELING REQUIREMENTS FOR HERDSHARE DISTRIBUTION:
  - Farm name and address (Southern Ridge Farm, 2402 Gene Fitzgerald Rd,
    Columbia TN 38401)
  - "RAW MILK — NOT PASTEURIZED" — must be prominent
  - "For herdshare members only — not for resale"
  - Date of collection (fill date)
  - Species (Cow)
  - A2A2 designation may be noted (this is a breed characteristic, not a
    regulatory label — but is accurate and valuable for members)

DO NOT USE:
  - Retail price labeling on raw milk containers
  - "For sale" language of any kind
  - A UPC barcode or retail-formatted label (implies retail sale)

HANDLING:
  - Raw milk should be refrigerated immediately after milking
  - Distribute to members in sealed, clean containers
  - Do not store raw milk in the same retail display case as products for sale
  - Do not accept raw milk returns from members

================================================================================
5. PASTEURIZED DAIRY — LOW-TEMP VAT PASTEURIZED
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
SRF also offers low-temperature vat pasteurized dairy products, which can be
sold directly to consumers without the herdshare structure.

LEGAL STATUS:
  - Pasteurized dairy products may be sold as retail items — no herdshare required
  - Must comply with TDA Grade A dairy standards if sold as fluid milk
  - Low-temp vat pasteurization (145°F for 30 minutes) is a recognized method
    under both TDA and federal PMO (Pasteurized Milk Ordinance) standards

TDA DAIRY PLANT LICENSE:
  - Any facility producing pasteurized dairy for sale must hold a TDA Dairy
    Plant License or operate under a licensed dairy's oversight
  - Confirm current license status with TDA: (615) 837-5100
  - Annual renewal required

LABELING FOR PASTEURIZED PRODUCTS:
  - Product name (e.g., "Whole Milk," "Heavy Cream")
  - "PASTEURIZED" statement
  - Grade designation if applicable ("Grade A")
  - Net volume
  - Farm name and address
  - Sell-by or use-by date
  - Keep refrigerated statement
  - Nutritional panel if packaged for retail sale (FDA labeling rules)
  - A2A2 claim is permissible as factual breed-characteristic information

================================================================================
6. A2A2 DAIRY CLAIM
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
A2A2 refers to the beta-casein protein variant present in the milk. Cows with
two copies of the A2 gene produce milk containing only the A2 beta-casein
protein, rather than the A1 protein found in most commercial dairy.

WHY SRF USES THIS CLAIM:
  - Many people who experience digestive discomfort with conventional dairy
    report tolerating A2A2 milk better
  - It's an accurate, verifiable genetic characteristic of the herd
  - It's a legitimate differentiator — not a marketing gimmick

REGULATORY STATUS:
  - "A2A2" is not a regulated claim under FDA or USDA rules as of April 2025
  - It is permissible as factual product information
  - Do not claim it "cures" lactose intolerance — it does not; lactose content
    is unaffected. The difference is in the protein structure, not the sugar.
  - SRF's accurate language: "A2A2 beta-casein protein — easier to digest
    for many people who have trouble with conventional dairy"
