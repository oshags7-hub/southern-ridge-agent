NOTE: Southern Ridge Farm operates as a hybrid farm/retail business. This creates

================================================================================
FORM 943 — AGRICULTURAL EMPLOYEES
================================================================================
Who files: Employers who paid wages to farmworkers
Form: IRS Form 943 (Employer's Annual Federal Tax Return for Agricultural Employees)
Filing frequency: Annual (due January 31 for prior year)
Deposit schedule: Depends on deposit liability — monthly or semiweekly

Applies to:
  - Workers paid to do farm work (planting, harvesting, animal care)
  - Workers paid $150+ in cash wages during the year, OR
  - Farm paid $2,500+ total to all farmworkers during the year

Special rule: H-2A workers are exempt from FICA (Social Security/Medicare)
Regular farm employees: Subject to FICA

================================================================================
FORM 941 — RETAIL / NON-FARM EMPLOYEES
================================================================================
Who files: Employers with non-agricultural employees
Form: IRS Form 941 (Employer's Quarterly Federal Tax Return)
Filing frequency: Quarterly (due April 30, July 31, Oct 31, Jan 31)

Applies to:
  - Farm stand retail workers (cashier, customer service)
  - Administrative / bookkeeping staff
  - Any worker NOT doing primary agricultural work

If an employee does both farm work AND retail work:
  - Allocate wages between Form 943 and Form 941 based on actual duties
  - Keep time records to support the split
  - Consult CPA for proper allocation method

================================================================================
FUTA — FEDERAL UNEMPLOYMENT TAX
================================================================================
Agricultural FUTA exemption applies if:
  - Paid less than $20,000 in cash wages to farm workers in any quarter, AND
  - Employed fewer than 10 workers on any given day in 20 different weeks

If either threshold is exceeded, FUTA applies to agricultural workers.
Retail workers are always subject to standard FUTA rules.

FUTA rate: 6% on first $7,000 of wages (reduced by state credit up to 5.4%)
Form: IRS Form 940 (annual, due January 31)

================================================================================
TENNESSEE SUTA — STATE UNEMPLOYMENT TAX
================================================================================
Issuing authority: Tennessee Department of Labor and Workforce Development (TDOL)
Rate: Varies by employer experience rating (new employers: assigned rate)
Wage base: First $7,000 of each employee's wages
Form: Quarterly report through Tennessee's Jobs4TN / TDOL portal
Due dates: Same quarters as Form 941

Agricultural exemption: Similar thresholds as FUTA — confirm with TDOL

TDOL contact: 844-224-5818
Portal: jobs4tn.gov

FILING DEADLINES CALENDAR
================================================================================
January 31:
  - Form 943 due (prior year farm employees)
  - Form 940 due (prior year FUTA)
  - W-2s to employees
  - W-3 + W-2 copies to SSA

April 30:  Form 941 Q1 due
July 31:   Form 941 Q2 due
October 31: Form 941 Q3 due
January 31: Form 941 Q4 due

Quarterly SUTA: Same schedule as Form 941

================================================================================
1099 REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================
Form 1099-NEC required for:
  - Any non-employee (contractor) paid $600+ during the year
  - Due to contractor: January 31
  - Due to IRS: January 31

Do NOT issue 1099s to: corporations (generally), employees (use W-2 instead)
