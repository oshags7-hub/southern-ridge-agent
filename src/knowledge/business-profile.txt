presence. Faith is woven into the farm's identity and is reflected in how the
family communicates with customers and runs the business.

BRAND VOICE
================================================================================
Tone: Warm, direct, unpretentious. Talks like a neighbor, not a brand.
Language level: Plain. No jargon. Short sentences.
We/I usage: "We" for the family/farm, "I" in personal email outreach.
Humor: Dry, occasional. Never forced.
Urgency: Real when needed (limited stock, harvest windows) — never manufactured.

What to avoid:
  - Corporate language ("leverage," "synergy," "ecosystem")
  - Over-explaining farming practices — the audience already respects them
  - Exclamation points in every sentence
  - Hashtag overload (3–5 max per Instagram post)

Content that performs well:
  - Behind-the-scenes moments (lambing, early morning, kids on the farm)
  - Honest product descriptions ("no fillers," "no shortcuts")
  - Specific details (pen 7, gate 4, east field) — makes it feel real
  - Scarcity when true (sold out by 9am)

FAITH INTEGRATION NOTES
================================================================================
Faith is a genuine part of the family's identity and is integrated naturally,
not performatively. Scripture references are used occasionally and fit the
content — never forced or preachy.

Examples of appropriate integration:
  - Closing a newsletter with a relevant verse
  - Referencing gratitude and stewardship in harvest or lambing posts
  - Using language like "blessed," "grateful," "good work"

Examples of inappropriate use:
  - Opening every post with a Bible verse
  - Preaching to customers about faith
  - Using faith language in invoices, receipts, or regulatory communications

Common verses used (natural fit):
  - Psalm 23:1 (shepherd content)
  - 1 Thessalonians 5:18 (gratitude / harvest)
  - Proverbs 12:11 (hard work / farm life)
