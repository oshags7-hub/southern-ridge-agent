SOUTHERN RIDGE FARM — PAYROLL & TAX REGULATORY GUIDE
Operational reference for compliance agents. Last updated: April 2025.
Source: IRS Publication 51 (Circular A), TDOL. Verify with CPA annually.

CRITICAL NOTE: Southern Ridge Farm is a hybrid farm/retail operation. This
creates a DUAL FILING REQUIREMENT — farm employees file under Form 943 (annual),
retail employees file under Form 941 (quarterly). Misclassifying employees
between these forms is a common audit trigger. Always confirm classification
with a CPA.

================================================================================
1. FORM 943 — AGRICULTURAL EMPLOYEES
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
IRS Form 943 (Employer's Annual Federal Tax Return for Agricultural Employees).
Used for workers who do farm labor — animal care, pasture work, processing
support, farm maintenance, field work.

WHAT IT REQUIRES IN PRACTICE:
  - Files ANNUALLY — due January 31 for the prior calendar year
  - Covers Social Security (6.2%), Medicare (1.45%) employer and employee shares
  - Federal income tax withholding on farm wages
  - Deposit schedule: monthly or semiweekly depending on prior-year tax liability
    — SRF's CPA should confirm the current deposit schedule

THRESHOLD: Form 943 applies if the farm paid:
  - $150 or more in cash wages to any one farmworker during the year, OR
  - $2,500 or more total to all farmworkers during the year

EMPLOYEE CLASSIFICATION FOR FORM 943:
  Covered: livestock care, pasture management, fencing, farm equipment operation,
  feeding, breeding, processing facility workers (when working as part of farm
  production), any on-farm labor that is directly agricultural in nature

  NOT covered: retail store employees at The Ridge, administrative staff,
  bookkeepers — these go on Form 941

DUAL-ROLE EMPLOYEES:
  If an employee does both farm work AND retail work (common at SRF), their
  wages must be allocated between Form 943 and Form 941 based on actual duties
  performed. Keep time records. Consult CPA on allocation method.

H-2A WORKERS (if applicable):
  H-2A agricultural visa workers are exempt from FICA (Social Security and
  Medicare). If SRF ever hires H-2A workers, those wages are reported on Form
  943 but FICA taxes are not withheld or matched.

PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
  - Failure to file: 5% of unpaid tax per month, up to 25%
  - Failure to deposit on time: graduated penalty 2%–15% based on how late
  - Trust fund recovery penalty: IRS can hold responsible persons personally
    liable for unpaid withholding taxes

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - Form 943 filed by January 31 each year
  - W-2s issued to all farm employees by January 31
  - W-3 transmittal + W-2 copies to SSA by January 31 (or February 28 if paper)
  - Deposit confirmation records (EFTPS confirmations)
  - Time records supporting agricultural classification for each employee

================================================================================
2. FORM 941 — RETAIL / NON-FARM EMPLOYEES
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
IRS Form 941 (Employer's Quarterly Federal Tax Return). Used for non-agricultural
employees — retail, administrative, customer service.

WHAT IT REQUIRES IN PRACTICE:
  - Files QUARTERLY — due April 30, July 31, October 31, January 31
  - Covers Social Security, Medicare, and federal income tax withholding
  - Same deposit rules as Form 943 (monthly or semiweekly based on liability)

EMPLOYEE CLASSIFICATION FOR FORM 941:
  Covered: The Ridge Butcher Shop employees, cashiers, butcher counter staff,
  retail floor workers, administrative and bookkeeping staff, marketing staff,
  any employee whose primary work is at a retail location or in an office context

IMPORTANT: The Ridge employees (1109 S Main St) are retail employees even if
  some of their work involves handling farm products. Their classification is
  determined by the nature of their employment, not the product they sell.

PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
  - Same structure as Form 943 — late filing and late deposit penalties apply
  - Each quarter is separate; missing one quarter compounds the issue

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - Form 941 filed each quarter (Q1 April 30, Q2 July 31, Q3 Oct 31, Q4 Jan 31)
  - W-2s to employees by January 31
  - Deposit records for each quarter

================================================================================
3. FUTA — FEDERAL UNEMPLOYMENT TAX
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
Federal Unemployment Tax Act (FUTA) funds unemployment benefits. Filed annually
on Form 940 (due January 31). Rate: 6% on first $7,000 of each employee's wages,
reduced by a state credit up to 5.4% (net effective rate typically 0.6%).

AGRICULTURAL FUTA EXEMPTION:
Farm employees are exempt from FUTA if BOTH of the following are true:
  - Farm paid less than $20,000 in cash wages to farm workers in every calendar
    quarter of the current and prior year, AND
  - Farm employed fewer than 10 workers on any given day in 20 or more different
    weeks during the current or prior year

If EITHER threshold is exceeded, FUTA applies to all farm employees.
Retail employees are always subject to standard FUTA rules — no exemption.

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - Form 940 filed annually by January 31
  - FUTA deposit records if quarterly liability exceeded $500
  - Payroll records supporting the employee count and wage figures

4. TENNESSEE SUTA — STATE UNEMPLOYMENT TAX
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
State Unemployment Tax Act (SUTA), administered by Tennessee Department of
Labor and Workforce Development (TDOL). Funds TN unemployment insurance.

WHAT IT REQUIRES:
  - Rate: Varies by employer's experience rating. New employers are assigned
    a standard rate — confirm current rate with TDOL or CPA
  - Wage base: First $7,000 of each employee's wages per year
  - Files quarterly — same schedule as Form 941
  - Filed through Tennessee's Jobs4TN portal (jobs4tn.gov)

AGRICULTURAL SUTA EXEMPTION:
  Similar thresholds as FUTA — confirm current TN-specific rules with TDOL or
  CPA. The exemption is not automatic; an analysis of employee count and wages
  is needed each year.

PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
  - Late payment: interest and penalties assessed by TDOL
  - Failure to register: TDOL may audit and assess back taxes

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - Quarterly SUTA reports filed via Jobs4TN portal
  - Payment confirmation records
  - Employee wage records by quarter

TDOL Contact: (844) 224-5818
Portal: jobs4tn.gov

5. W-2 AND YEAR-END FILINGS
================================================================================
WHAT IS REQUIRED:
  - W-2 to each employee: by January 31
  - W-3 transmittal + W-2 copies to Social Security Administration: by January 31
    (electronically) or February 28 (paper — avoid paper for more than a few W-2s)
  - Both farm employees (943) and retail employees (941) receive W-2s

COMMON ERRORS TO AVOID:
  - Forgetting to include box 12 codes (e.g., retirement plan contributions)
  - Incorrect EIN on W-2s
  - Mismatched names vs. SSA records — use employee's legal name as on their
    Social Security card

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - Copy B retained with farm payroll records for at least 4 years
  - W-3 and proof of SSA submission

6. 1099-NEC — INDEPENDENT CONTRACTORS
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
If SRF pays any non-employee (contractor, freelancer, vendor providing services)
$600 or more during the calendar year, a Form 1099-NEC must be issued.

WHEN IT APPLIES AT SRF:
  - Freelance photographers, social media contractors
  - Veterinarians (if not incorporated — confirm)
  - Contract labor for farm or butcher shop work
  - Any service provider who is not an employee and received $600+

WHEN IT DOES NOT APPLY:
  - Payments to incorporated companies (S corps, C corps) — generally exempt
  - Payments for goods (not services)
  - Employees — they receive W-2s, not 1099s

DEADLINES:
  - Form 1099-NEC to contractor: January 31
  - Form 1099-NEC to IRS: January 31

PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
  - $50–$580 per form not filed (depending on how late), with no cap for
    intentional disregard

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - W-9 form collected from every contractor before first payment
  - Payment records by contractor for the year

7. TENNESSEE STATE INCOME TAX WITHHOLDING
================================================================================
NOTE: Tennessee has no state income tax on wages (Hall Income Tax applied only
to investment income and was fully phased out as of January 1, 2021). No state
income tax withholding from employee wages is required in Tennessee. This is
a significant simplification vs. most other states.

9. WORKER CLASSIFICATION — EMPLOYEE VS. CONTRACTOR
================================================================================
RISK AREA: Misclassifying employees as independent contractors is one of the most
common and expensive IRS/DOL audit findings for small farms and retail businesses.

THE IRS COMMON LAW TEST:
  A worker is likely an EMPLOYEE (not contractor) if the business:
  - Controls what work is done AND how it is done
  - Sets the worker's schedule
  - Provides the tools and equipment
  - Has an ongoing relationship with the worker
  - Prohibits the worker from doing the same work for others

  Independent contractors typically: set their own hours, use their own tools,
  take on multiple clients, and have the ability to profit or lose money on the job.

AT SRF: Most farm workers and butcher shop workers will be employees, not
  contractors. Occasional freelance photographers, web developers, and consultants
  may qualify as contractors — collect a W-9 and confirm with CPA.
