SOUTHERN RIDGE FARM — MEAT REGULATORY GUIDE
Operational reference for compliance agents. Last updated: April 2025.

================================================================================
1. TDA RETAIL MEAT SALES PERMIT
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
Issued by the Tennessee Department of Agriculture (TDA). Required for any
establishment that sells meat directly to consumers at retail.

WHAT IT REQUIRES IN PRACTICE:
  - Annual permit renewal (permit year runs January–December)
  - Physical permit must be posted visibly at each retail location
  - Applies to both The Ridge Butcher Shop and the farm store
  - Facility must pass TDA inspection to obtain and retain permit
  - Meat sold must come from USDA-inspected or TDA-inspected sources
  - Staff handling meat must follow food safety practices (see cold chain below)

PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
  - Operating without a permit: civil penalty up to $1,000/day
  - Permit suspension or revocation for repeated violations
  - Potential criminal referral for willful violations

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - Current permit certificate (posted at facility)
  - Renewal application submitted before December 31 each year
  - Inspection records from TDA site visits
  - Proof of USDA inspection of processed product (see USDA section)

CONTACT: Tennessee Department of Agriculture
  Consumer and Industry Services: (615) 837-5100
  Website: tn.gov/agriculture

2. TDA RETAIL FOOD STORE LICENSE
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
Separate from the meat permit — covers The Ridge as a retail food store selling
a broader range of food products (baked goods, dairy, eggs, ferments, etc.).

WHAT IT REQUIRES IN PRACTICE:
  - Annual license renewal
  - Covers sale of any food item, not just meat
  - Facility must meet TDA food establishment requirements:
    — Adequate refrigeration for perishables
    — Proper hand-washing stations
    — Food contact surfaces that are cleanable and sanitizable
    — Pest control measures in place
    — Adequate lighting and ventilation
  - Applies to The Ridge location; farm store may fall under separate
    agricultural exemptions for on-farm sales — confirm annually with TDA

PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
  - Fines, license suspension, or closure order
  - Public posting of violations (reputational risk)

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - Current license certificate posted at The Ridge
  - Renewal submitted annually
  - Facility inspection reports on file

3. USDA FSIS FEDERAL INSPECTION
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
Southern Ridge Farm operates an on-farm USDA FSIS (Food Safety and Inspection
Service) inspected processing facility. This is the federal requirement for any
meat that crosses state lines or is sold commercially under the USDA mark.

WHAT IT REQUIRES IN PRACTICE:
  - A USDA inspector must be present during all slaughter and processing
    operations — inspection is continuous for slaughter, periodic for processing
  - All meat must pass ante-mortem (live animal) and post-mortem (carcass)
    inspection before it can be passed for human consumption
  - Only USDA-passed product may bear the USDA inspection mark
  - Facility must meet FSIS construction and sanitation standards:
    — Separate clean and dirty areas
    — Temperature-controlled processing rooms
    — Documented sanitation procedures (SSOPs — Sanitation Standard Operating
      Procedures) written and on file
  - Daily sanitation records must be maintained
  - Rejected product ("condemned") must be disposed of per FSIS protocol —
    never re-enter the food supply

PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
  - FSIS can suspend inspection (effectively shutting down the facility)
  - Criminal prosecution under the Federal Meat Inspection Act for willful
    adulteration or misbranding
  - Civil penalties and facility delisting

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - USDA Grant of Inspection (establishment number assigned by FSIS)
  - Written SSOPs — must be reviewed and updated annually or when process changes
  - Daily pre-operational and operational sanitation records
  - Ante-mortem and post-mortem inspection logs (maintained by inspector, copies
    kept on file)
  - Condemned product disposition records

CONTACT: USDA FSIS District Office (Nashville area)
  FSIS Meat and Poultry Hotline: 1-888-MPHotline (1-888-674-6854)
  FSIS website: fsis.usda.gov

4. HACCP (HAZARD ANALYSIS AND CRITICAL CONTROL POINTS)
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
Federal mandate under 9 CFR Part 417 for all USDA-inspected establishments.
A systematic approach to identifying and controlling food safety hazards
(biological, chemical, physical) in the processing flow.

WHAT IT REQUIRES IN PRACTICE:
  - Written HACCP plan covering every product category processed at the facility
  - Seven HACCP principles must be documented:
    1. Conduct hazard analysis (identify what could go wrong at each step)
    2. Identify Critical Control Points (CCPs) — steps where control is essential
    3. Establish critical limits at each CCP (e.g., internal temp for cooking)
    4. Establish monitoring procedures (who checks, how often, what equipment)
    5. Establish corrective actions when a CCP is out of control
    6. Verification procedures (validating the system works)
    7. Recordkeeping and documentation
  - HACCP plan must be reviewed at least annually, or whenever:
    — A new product is added
    — A new process step is introduced
    — A food safety problem is identified
    — New hazard information emerges
  - At minimum one person trained in HACCP principles (by HACCP training course)
    must be employed at the facility

COMMON CCPs FOR SRF OPERATIONS:
  - Chilling: carcass must reach 40°F within specified time after slaughter
  - Cold storage: product must be maintained at 40°F or below (refrigerated)
    or 0°F or below (frozen)
  - Cooking/smoking: internal temperature requirements for cooked products
    (pork sausage: 160°F internal; poultry products: 165°F internal)
  - Antimicrobial interventions (e.g., organic acid rinse on carcasses)

PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
  - FSIS can issue a Noncompliance Record (NR) for each deviation
  - Pattern of NRs can trigger Notice of Intended Enforcement (NOIE)
  - Continued non-compliance leads to suspension of inspection

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - Written HACCP plan (signed and dated by responsible establishment official)
  - CCP monitoring records (daily or per-lot, depending on the CCP)
  - Corrective action records for any deviations
  - Verification records (calibration logs, periodic product testing)
  - Annual HACCP reassessment signature page

5. LABELING REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
All meat products bearing the USDA mark must be labeled per FSIS labeling
regulations (9 CFR Parts 316-317 for meat; 381 for poultry).

WHAT IT REQUIRES IN PRACTICE:
  - Product name (common or usual name)
  - Inspection legend and establishment number
  - Net weight (on consumer packages)
  - Ingredients statement (for processed/multi-ingredient products like sausage)
  - Allergen declarations where required
  - Handling instructions: "KEEP REFRIGERATED" or "KEEP FROZEN" as applicable
  - Safe handling instructions panel (required on most raw meat packages)
  - For "grass-fed" claim: must comply with USDA AMS standard (100% grass/forage,
    no grain supplementation) — SRF meets this; documentation of grazing
    practices should be maintained in case of challenge
  - For "no hormones" claim on beef: must be accompanied by statement that
    federal regulations prohibit hormone use in poultry and pork on those labels
  - For "no antibiotics" or "raised without antibiotics": requires affidavit
    on file with FSIS; label claim must be approved before use

PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
  - Misbranded product is adulterated under the FMIA — can be seized
  - Unapproved label claims: FSIS can require label recall and correction

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - Approved label sketches (or generic approval documentation) on file
  - Affidavits supporting "no antibiotics," "no hormones" claims
  - If using third-party claim (e.g., "Animal Welfare Approved"): current
    certification documentation on file

6. COLD CHAIN REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
Temperature control from processing through sale. Both FSIS regulations and
TDA retail requirements specify temperature limits.

WHAT IT REQUIRES IN PRACTICE:
  - Refrigerated raw meat: 40°F or below at all times after processing
  - Frozen meat: 0°F or below; once frozen, do not re-freeze after thawing
    unless re-processed under inspection
  - Display cases at The Ridge: must maintain 40°F or below
  - Cooler/freezer logs: record temps at least once per day; twice recommended
  - If a cooler fails: document the event, assess product safety, take corrective
    action (transfer product, discard if temperature abuse is confirmed)
  - Customer advisory for bulk cow shares: product is frozen for pickup;
    advise customers to bring a cooler for trips longer than 30 minutes

TEMPERATURE QUICK REFERENCE:
  - Refrigerated raw product: 28-40°F (target range)
  - Frozen: 0°F or below
  - Cooked/ready-to-eat hot-hold: 140°F or above
  - Cooling cooked product: to 70°F within 2 hours, then 40°F within 4 hours total
  - Ground beef: cook to 160°F internal

PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
  - Temperature abuse is a food safety adulteration issue
  - FSIS or TDA can condemn product and require disposal
  - Repeated violations trigger facility-level enforcement action

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - Daily cooler and freezer temperature logs at The Ridge and farm store
  - Calibration records for thermometers used in monitoring
  - Corrective action records for any temperature deviations

7. TRANSPORT RULES
================================================================================
WHAT IT IS:
Rules governing how meat is transported between the farm processing facility,
The Ridge, and customers.

WHAT IT REQUIRES IN PRACTICE:
  - Transport vehicles carrying raw meat must maintain 40°F or below
    (refrigerated) or 0°F or below (frozen) during transit
  - Vehicles used for meat transport must be clean and free of contamination
    sources (chemicals, non-food items)
  - Market Wagon and Nashville farmers market deliveries: product must be
    maintained at proper temperature during transport and at the pickup location
  - Bulk cow share pickup: farm ensures product is frozen and packaged;
    customer assumes responsibility after pickup

INTRASTATE VS. INTERSTATE:
  - SRF primarily sells within Tennessee (intrastate) — USDA inspection still
    applies because SRF chose federal inspection (also required for any
    interstate commerce and for the USDA mark)
  - Any out-of-state shipment: USDA inspection mark is required; product may not
    leave Tennessee without it (SRF's facility meets this requirement)

DOCUMENTATION NEEDED:
  - Temperature logs for delivery vehicles if delivering regularly to markets
  - Delivery records for Market Wagon (platform maintains these)
  - For direct customer delivery: maintain records of delivery date, product,
    and customer
